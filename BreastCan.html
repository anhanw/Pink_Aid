Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a disease caused by uncontrollable growth of cells in the breast. The type of breast cancer is determined by the cells that turn into cancer, and it can begin in different parts of the breast. The three main parts of a breast are lobules, ducts, and connective tissue. Most breast cancers start in the ducts or lobules. If the cancer cells spread beyond the breast to other parts of the body through blood and lymph vessels, it will result in metastasis, which is much more dangerous and serious.


Symptom

Breast cancer may not have symptoms in its early stages. As the cancer grows, it may cause noticeable changes in the breast, such as a lump, thickening, or changes to the skin or nipple. The most common symptom of ductal carcinoma is a firm or hard lump that feels different from the rest of the breast, while lobular carcinoma may cause a fullness or swelling in the breast or a change in the breast's texture or appearance. Early detection and treatment of breast cancer can improve outcomes and increase the chances of survival. Late stage will present severe symptoms including bone pain, weight loss, nausea, jaundice, headache, muscle weakness.

Precautions

Regular screening is important: Women should follow the recommended guidelines for breast cancer screening, including regular mammograms, clinical breast exams, and self-exams in order to discover the disease in an earlier stage.
